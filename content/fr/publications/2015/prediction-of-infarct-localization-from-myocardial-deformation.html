Non-linear dimensionality reduction is used to estimate the Euclidean space of coordinates encoding deformation patterns. The infarct location of a new subject is inferred by two consecutive interpolations, formulated as multiscale kernel regressions. They consist in (i) finding the low-dimensional coordinates associated to the measured deformation pattern, and (ii) estimating the possible infarct location associated to these coordinates. These concepts were tested on a database of 500 synthetic cases generated from a realistic electromechanical model of the two ventricles. The database consisted of infarcts of random extent, shape, and location overlapping the whole left-anterior-descending coronary territory.
